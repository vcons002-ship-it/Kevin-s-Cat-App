1. Camera (where to watch for a person)

Camera not listed? Enter it manually

How to find this: open your camera's app or its web page to find the RTSP URL (often rtsp://<camera-ip>:554/...). The camera IP is in your router's device list or the camera app. Auto-detect uses ONVIF — if your camera isn't ONVIF or needs a login, type the URL and credentials here.
Main vs sub feed: the high-res main stream spots distant cats better but uses more CPU; the sub stream is lighter. Paste whichever URL you prefer — then tune "Detection detail" and "Scan rate" below to balance quality vs CPU.

2. Speaker(s) (where the chime plays)

Found automatically over WiFi (Google Cast — no account or login needed). Pick one or more — Ctrl/Cmd-click (or drag) to select several and the treat plays on all of them. This device and the speakers must be on the same WiFi network. Names match your Google Home app. Connections are kept open so there's no "connecting" chime each time.

5. Quiet time (optional — no chimes during this window)

During these hours the app still watches and logs detections, but won't play the chime. Leave both blank to disable. A window like 22:00 → 07:00 correctly spans midnight.

6. Region of interest (optional — only watch part of the view)

Click Grab frame to pull a still from the camera, then drag a box over the area to watch (e.g. the kitchen floor). Everything outside the box is ignored — this cuts false positives and, by zooming in, helps spot distant cats. Leave unset to watch the whole frame.
