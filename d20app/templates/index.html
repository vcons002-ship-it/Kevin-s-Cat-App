1. Camera (where to watch for a person)

Add a camera / enter one manually

How to find this: open your camera's app or its web page to find the RTSP URL (often rtsp://<camera-ip>:554/...). The camera IP is in your router's device list or the camera app. Auto-detect uses ONVIF — if your camera isn't ONVIF or needs a login, type the URL and credentials here.
Save camera adds it (with its login) to the Saved cameras dropdown above so you can switch between cameras quickly. Credentials are stored locally in config.yaml in plain text.
Main vs sub feed: the high-res main stream spots distant cats better but uses more CPU; the sub stream is lighter. Paste whichever URL you prefer — then tune "Detection detail" and "Scan rate" below to balance quality vs CPU.

2. Speaker(s) (where the chime plays)

Found automatically over WiFi (Google Cast — no account or login needed). Pick one or more — Ctrl/Cmd-click (or drag) to select several and the treat plays on all of them. This device and the speakers must be on the same WiFi network. Names match your Google Home app. Connections are kept open so there's no "connecting" chime each time. The first option, "This PC (local audio)", plays the sound on this computer's own speakers (handy on a laptop/desktop; needs the optional local-audio support from setup) — you can pick it, a Cast device, or both.

Loops a silent clip every couple of minutes so the Cast receiver never unloads — the only thing that actually stops the chime on a Google Home. Trade-off: it holds the speaker active and briefly yields to (won't stomp on) other audio. Leave off if you use these speakers for music.

5. Motion & CPU (cut false triggers and save power)

CPU: big saving — skips the neural net between rolls (nothing it sees can trigger anyway) and resumes automatically just before the next window, so no treat is missed.
Advanced motion tuning

These three are exactly what the Motion sensitivity preset sets for you — editing any of them switches the preset to Custom. Raise them if the camera keeps firing on shadows, changing light, or a swaying plant; lower them to catch smaller or more distant movement. None of these change CPU much on their own, but fewer false triggers means the neural net runs less often.

6. Quiet time (optional — no chimes during this window)

During these hours the app still watches and logs detections, but won't play the chime. Leave both blank to disable. A window like 22:00 → 07:00 correctly spans midnight.

7. Region of interest (optional — only watch part of the view)

Click Grab frame to pull a still from the camera, then drag a box over the area to watch (e.g. the kitchen floor). Everything outside the box is ignored — this cuts false positives and, by zooming in, helps spot distant cats. Leave unset to watch the whole frame.

Live detection

Start watching to see the live feed.

A real-time view of what the detector sees, with boxes drawn around any person (green) or cat (orange) as it recognises them. It streams only while watching and only to browsers with this open, so it costs little — turn Show live feed off on a slow connection.
Smooth feed runs a dedicated capture thread so the video plays at the camera's frame rate instead of stuttering at your scan rate (which is gated by detection speed — heavy on a slow CPU / big model). It costs a little extra CPU and reads the camera continuously; leave it off if you only need the occasional frame. Smoothness is still limited by the camera's actual output rate.
